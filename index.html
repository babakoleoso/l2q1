Single Week Lesson

ÌMÚLE`

The Sacred Foundation of Alignment
A SwamiG Institute Iyawo lesson on grounding, spiritual stability, and the conscious establishment of a foundation strong enough to support devotion, speech, wisdom, and legacy.

“What you build will only rise to the level of what it stands upon.”

Lesson Focus

Ìmúle` is the act of consciously establishing one’s foundation so that the Ori can function with clarity, steadiness, and authority. In this lesson, grounding is not treated as a casual mood, but as a sacred discipline. Before one can speak with power, interpret with wisdom, or walk in alignment, one must first be firmly established.

SGI Pillars

Pillar One

Radical Devotion
(Auset / Yemojah)

Radical Devotion teaches that foundation is built through consistency, surrender, and sacred discipline. To establish Ìmúle` is to return, daily and intentionally, to the practice of aligning the self.

Devotion is not merely emotion. It is the willingness to show up, to bow inwardly, and to build strength through repetition. In this way, grounding becomes an act of reverence toward Ori.

Without devotion, there is no true foundation—only unanchored intention.
Pillar Two

Words of Power
(Sekert / Olokun)

Words of Power teaches that speech stabilizes reality. The spoken word, when used consciously, helps anchor the inner condition and shape the field in which spiritual work unfolds.

In the practice of Ìmúle`, declaration is not ornamental. It is functional. The words spoken during grounding serve as the bridge between inner intention and manifested condition.

What is spoken with consciousness begins to organize what was formerly scattered.
Pillar Three

Infinite Wisdom
(Tehuti / Orunmila)

Infinite Wisdom teaches that correct perception requires truthfulness. One must not assume balance, but verify it. One must not pretend clarity, but examine condition honestly.

Through Ìmúle`, the seeker learns to observe the true state of mind, body, and spirit. Wisdom begins where self-deception ends.

Foundation deepens when awareness becomes honest enough to see what is truly present.

SGI Principles

Principle A

Extending Royal Legacy

Ìmúle` is not only personal stability. It is the strengthening of one’s role in an ongoing lineage of disciplined spiritual becoming. Every deliberate act of grounding reinforces the dignity, order, and continuity of what has been entrusted to you.

To establish foundation is to prepare oneself to carry legacy in a worthy way. What is unstable cannot transmit royalty with integrity.

Principle B

Standing on the Shoulders of Enlightened Ancestors

No sincere grounding practice is ever performed alone. Through conscious stillness, remembrance, and alignment, the student comes into relationship with those whose discipline and illumination created the path now being walked.

Ìmúle` therefore becomes an ancestral act. You establish yourself by consciously standing upon wisdom already earned, embodied, and passed forward.

Words of Power for This Lesson

This statement may be spoken slowly, with reverence, at the beginning of practice:

“Ori mi, I establish my foundation in truth.
I am grounded. I am aligned. I am stable.
I stand upon the wisdom of enlightened ancestors.”

Core Practice — Establishing Ìmúle`

1

Position the Body-Temple

Sit or stand intentionally. Keep the spine upright and the body composed. Let the feet connect consciously with the ground. Feel the body as temple, pillar, and vessel.

2

Regulate the Breath

Inhale for a count of four, hold for four, and exhale for four. Repeat for several rounds until the body begins to settle and the mind becomes less scattered.

3

Speak the Foundation

Use the lesson’s Words of Power with intention. Speak clearly and without haste. Let each line descend into the body rather than remain only at the lips.

4

Enter Stillness

Remain in silence for several minutes. Observe what is present without immediately correcting, decorating, or resisting it. Return to conscious breating. Allow truth to reveal condition.

5

Verify the Condition

Ask inwardly: “What is my condition?” Notice whether the self feels stable, resistant, receptive, mixed, or unsettled. Infinite Wisdom begins in accurate observation.

Weekly Assignment

The Discipline of Foundation

Establish and perform a personal Ìmúle` ritual for your Ori during this lesson period. The purpose is to cultivate steadiness through devotion, speech, and truthful observation.

  • Select a consistent day and time for the practice.
  • Choose a location that supports calmness, order, and sacred focus.
  • Include body position, breath regulation, spoken Words of Power, and stillness.
  • Practice for approximately 5–10 minutes each day during the assigned week.

After each practice, document your state before and after, along with any notable insight regarding your level of grounding, alignment, or resistance.

Reflection Prompt: “How did Radical Devotion, Words of Power, and Infinite Wisdom strengthen my foundation during this week of Ìmúle`?”

Teaching Insight

Ìmúle` is the silent prerequisite to accurate perception. Where there is no grounding, devotion becomes irregular, words lose force, and wisdom becomes distorted by instability. When foundation is properly established, however, the student becomes more capable of clarity, discipline, and right interpretation.

In this sense, Ìmúle` is not the end of spiritual work. It is the condition that makes deeper work trustworthy.

“Establish your foundation daily.
Speak your alignment into existence.
Stand in wisdom.
And extend royal legacy while standing on the shoulders of enlightened ancestors.”
